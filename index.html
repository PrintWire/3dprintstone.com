The Materials

Where Geology Meets Algorithm

Stone-like materials — terracotta, fireclay, geopolymer, and aggregate composites — represent the oldest human use of ceramics and the newest frontier of additive manufacturing.

Today's stone printing spans from robotic concrete extrusion at architectural scale to precision fireclay components for kiln and furnace applications. We cover the full range.

Architectural Facades

Terracotta and geopolymer facade panels with custom surface relief — parametrically generated and kiln-fired.

Refractory Industrial

Fireclay and high-alumina components for kilns, furnaces, and high-temperature industrial processes.

Landscape & Hardscape

Public Sculpture

Monumental concrete and stone composite sculpture for public plazas — printed robotically, fired or geopolymer-cured.
